Morristown Horse Property

Morristown sits approximately 20 miles east of Wickenburg on Highway 60, in Maricopa County's unincorporated rural corridor. It is the value alternative to Wickenburg proper for buyers who want horse-zoned acreage in the same general region but at price points that have not yet caught up with the Wickenburg market's appreciation. The terrain is similar — flat to gently rolling Sonoran Desert, sandy alluvial soil, and the open sky that characterizes western Maricopa County — but the prices are meaningfully lower, and the driving distance from Phoenix is 40 minutes rather than 60.

The buyer profile for Morristown skews toward Phoenix-area horse owners who are relocating or expanding their horse operations and need the Phoenix metro within a reasonable commute. Morristown properties consistently offer 20 to 30 percent more land and improved square footage per dollar than comparable Wickenburg properties, and the Maricopa County agricultural zoning framework applies cleanly throughout the area.

Property Types and Pricing

Morristown horse properties range from 2-acre ranchettes with modest homes and basic horse facilities at $280,000 to $400,000, to established 10-to-20-acre operations with multi-stall barns and working arenas at $550,000 to $700,000. Large-acreage parcels of 40 acres or more in the Morristown-to-Wittmann corridor are available at $2,000 to $4,000 per acre — below even Congress area pricing for remote parcels.

The tradeoff relative to Wickenburg is community depth. Morristown does not have the equestrian infrastructure concentration that Wickenburg offers — fewer working trainers nearby, less developed event culture, and a more dispersed horse-owning population. For buyers whose primary priority is facility value and proximity to Phoenix rather than an immersive equestrian community, Morristown delivers. For buyers who want to be in the team roping community, attending local events, and surrounded by serious horse people, Wickenburg proper is the answer.

Key Takeaways

  • 20 miles east of Wickenburg on Highway 60 — 40 minutes from Phoenix.
  • 20–30% more property per dollar than comparable Wickenburg parcels.
  • Typical price range: $280,000 to $700,000 for horse properties on 2–20 acres.
  • Maricopa County agricultural zoning applies throughout — straightforward for horse operations.
  • Lower community depth than Wickenburg proper — better value buy if location matters more than equestrian culture.
